GlucoCure Hydrogel: a living gel that turns a wound's own sugar into healingWound care · preclinical

An early-stage wound dressing for chronic and diabetic wounds, designed to feed on the excess sugar that feeds infection.

Chronic and diabetic wounds are hard to heal for a frustrating reason: they are flooded with excess glucose which is a feast for bacteria, so infection takes hold and the wound gets stuck, unable to close.

GlucoCure HydrogelA soft, water-rich gel that sits gently on a wound to keep it moist and protected. is a soft gel dressing with a twist. Living inside it are friendly, body-compatible microbes that treat all that surplus glucose as fuel. As they consume the very sugar that bacteria rely on, they continuously release natural antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory compounds that help keep the wound clean and calm.

Because the microbes keep working rather than releasing a single dose that quickly fades, the idea is a steady, lasting effect over the days a wound needs to recover.

In preclinical testing, GlucoCure has shown significantly faster wound closure in diabetic rat and porcine models, eradication of MRSA and P. aeruginosa with biofilm prevention, a shift toward anti-inflammatory (M1→M2) immune remodeling, and upregulated VEGF-mediated angiogenesis. This work was shaped by real-world clinical insight from Virtus Medical Group. GlucoCure is a research-stage product — not approved, not proven in humans, and nothing here is medical advice.

It turns the wound's own problem — excess sugar — into the fuel for its cure.
