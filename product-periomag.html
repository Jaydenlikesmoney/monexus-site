Gum disease is almost universal. We're designing a kinder way to fight it.Oral care · preclinical

PerioMag is an early-stage idea: living probiotics guided deep under the gumline by a clear, retainer-like aligner — designed to keep working long after a single dose.

Almost everyone runs into gum disease. By some counts, advanced gum disease touches around 90% of people worldwide — and it doesn't stay in your mouth. Researchers have linked it to heart and lung disease, diabetes, Alzheimer's, some cancers, and problems in pregnancy. Yet the care we have today asks a lot of people: repeated dental visits, costly rounds of treatment, and procedures that can hurt enough that many of us quietly put them off.

There's also a problem. The damage hides in narrow pockets deep between tooth and gum, where ordinary medicines struggle to reach — and the thin fluid that constantly seeps through those pockets washes most of a drug away before it can finish the job. PerioMag takes a different route. Instead of a single dose that bursts and then fades, it uses living probiotic microbes held inside an engineered material that behaves like a tiny, self-renewing factory, steadily making the compounds that fight infection and calm inflammation. Gentle magnets are meant to guide these microbes down into the pockets that normal treatment fails to reach.

The idea is for it to feel familiar. You would wear a personalized magnetic aligner — much like the clear retainer many people already know. In an early rat study, a single application settled deep, kept releasing, and the animals' gum disease resolved over about four weeks. We want to be honest about where this stands.
